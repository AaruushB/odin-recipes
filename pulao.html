Goan Prawn Pulao

Description

Goan Prawn Pulao is a fragrant one-pot rice dish from Goa (India’s beach-side foodie heaven). It pairs juicy prawns with aromatic basmati rice and warm spices for comfort in every bite. It’s not super heavy like biryani — it’s lighter, bright, and perfect for everyday dinner vibes

Ingredients Needed

  • ¼ pound prawns, peeled and deveined
  • sea salt to taste
  • ¼ cup grated coconut
  • 2 Kashmiri chili peppers
  • ½ tablespoon coriander seeds
  • 1 ½ cloves garlic, peeled
  • 2 ½ peppercorns
  • ½ tablespoon vegetable oil
  • ½ small onion, sliced
  • ⅛ teaspoon ground turmeric
  • ¾ cup water, or as needed

Directions

  1. Season prawns with sea salt.
  2. Grind coconut, chile peppers, coriander seeds, garlic, and peppercorns together using a mortar and pestle until masala is evenly orange.
  3. Heat oil in a pot over medium heat; cook and stir onion until softened and lightly browned, 5 to 10 minutes. Mix masala and turmeric into onion; cook until fragrant, about 1 minute. Add enough water to make a substantial and smooth gravy.
  4. Bring gravy to a boil; add prawns and okra and cook until prawns are cooked through, about 10 minutes. Stir kokum into prawn mixture and return to a boil. Remove pot from heat and let stand.
